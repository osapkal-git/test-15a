Gamma Ray:
The gamma-ray spectrometer uses a high-purified germanium crystal to capture gamma rays emitted by asteroid Psyche. The gamma rays are like a fingerprint. Every element on the periodic table releases gamma rays of a specific energy. As the gamma-ray spectrometer collects the gamma rays from orbit, it can determine what element makes up the asteroid.

Neutron Spectrometer:
Neutrons provide information about particular element like iron, nickel and hydrogen that complement the gamma-ray measurements. The Neutron Spectrometer uses three cylinders to capture neutrons with three specialized detectors called helium-3 sensors which detect different energy ranges of neutrons to reveal distinct information about asteroid Psyche’s composition.
